About

Winny is a passionate climate justice activist and a fierce defender of Queer Human Rights. She is the Founding Director of ICA Foundation Kenya, a bold and visionary LBQ-led climate justice organization working at the intersection of climate change, gender justice, and human rights. With a strong background in LBQ organizing, feminist movement building, and climate policy advocacy, Winny brings a wealth of experience and leadership to local, national, and global platforms. She is a leader and mentor with the Africa Climate Reality Project, having been trained in both Cape Town, South Africa and Nairobi, Kenya.

In 2024, she joined other young feminists as a COP29 delegate under MADRE and the Rosa Luxemburg Stiftung (RLS), where she moderated a high-level session at the Resilience Hub in Baku, Azerbaijan, co-hosted by Americares, UN OCHA, ICCCAD, and other global partners. Winny represented YOUNGO Health Working Group at the First Africa Climate Summit that was held in Nairobi in September, 2023.

Winny’s activism is rooted in her commitment to building inclusive, community-centered climate action that centers the leadership and lived realities of marginalized groups especially Lesbian, Bisexual, and Queer (LBQ) individuals who are often left out of climate discourse and decision-making. Through ICA Foundation Kenya, she founded the Kenya's LBQ Climate Action Taskforce, a groundbreaking initiative that brings together young queer climate and human rights activists in Kenya to foster inclusive policy advocacy at the national level.

Outside advocacy, Winny is a Web Developer. She leads a skills-building program for Queer youths on tech in Kenya, to create employment and co-create climate solutions using Queer-centered technology, experience and knowledge.
